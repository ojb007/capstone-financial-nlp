Title: Weighing Nvidia (NVDA) Valuation After Recent Share Price Softness And Mixed Signals On Growth
Source: Simply Wall St / Yahoo Finance
Date: Mar 18, 2026
Ticker: NVDA

Recent performance snapshot for NVIDIA (NVDA)

NVIDIA has drawn fresh attention after mixed short-term share performance, with a 1-day return of about a 1% decline, a 7-day return near a 2% decline, and a modest move over the past month.

Over the past 3 months the stock shows a small positive return of roughly 4%, while year-to-date performance sits around a 4% decline, creating an interesting contrast with its 1-year total return above 50%.

Recent share price softness contrasts with a very strong multi-year total shareholder return. This suggests sentiment has cooled in the short term while longer-term holders have still seen substantial gains.

With NVIDIA now carrying a market value above US$4,000b, strong reported revenue and net income, and a sizeable gap to analyst price targets, it is reasonable to ask whether there is still a buying opportunity here or whether the market is already pricing in future growth.

Most Popular Narrative: 6.9% Overvalued

Based on the most-followed narrative fair value of US$170.26 compared with the last close of US$181.93, NVIDIA screens as modestly expensive according to KiwiInvest.

The narrative argues that Nvidia could hit US$400b in annual revenue in five years’ time, with around 90% of revenue coming from data center customers. This equates to roughly US$90b per quarter, or about 30,000 Blackwell racks at approximately US$3m per rack.

This view depends on ambitious data center demand, rich margins, and a premium future earnings multiple.

Result: Fair Value of US$170.26 (OVERVALUED)

However, this story can change quickly if a cheaper AI platform undercuts CUDA, or if regulation limits data center power use and restricts demand.

Another angle on NVIDIA's valuation

The user narrative pegs fair value at US$170.26 per share, suggesting NVIDIA is 6.9% overvalued versus the US$181.93 last close. A P/E-based view tells a different story.

At 36.8x earnings, NVIDIA trades below the U.S. semiconductor industry average of 41.7x and below peers at 87.1x.

The company also sits under an estimated fair ratio of 58x, which is a level the market could move toward over time. That gap can be read in two ways: as valuation risk if expectations reset, or as headroom if earnings keep pace with current forecasts.
